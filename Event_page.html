Ratnagiri Space Apps Challenge

Ratnagiri's Future: Innovating with Space, Science & Technology

About the Event

Welcome to the **Ratnagiri Space Apps Challenge**, a local hackathon designed to inspire high school and college students to innovate using real-world space data. Under the theme of "Ratnagiri's Future: Innovating with Space, Science & Technology," participants will form teams to tackle challenges related to their community, such as smart farming, urban planning, environmental preservation, and coastal monitoring. The event's goal is to foster a new generation of problem-solvers by encouraging collaboration, creativity, and critical thinking. Participants will have the opportunity to use free and open data from NASA and other space agency partners to develop project solutions, with the top projects being nominated for judging at the global level.

Event Dates: October 4-5, 2025
Target Audience: High school & Bachelor's students
Location: Ratnagiri, India

Challenges

Smart Farming for Ratnagiri's Farms

This challenge asks participants to develop a solution using NASA satellite data and other open-source resources to address key agricultural issues in the Ratnagiri region. Solutions could include mobile apps, data dashboards, or predictive models that provide farmers with actionable insights on weather patterns, soil health, and crop stress to improve yields and sustainability.

Urban Planning for Sustainable Growth

Participants will leverage NASA Earth observation data (like imagery from Landsat or MODIS) to analyze and visualize urban expansion in Ratnagiri. The goal is to create a tool or a plan that helps city planners manage sustainable growth, monitor changes in land use, identify potential for "urban heat islands," or plan for future infrastructure development.

Preservation of Ratnagiri's Natural Heritage

This challenge focuses on using NASA and other geospatial data to monitor and protect the region's rich biodiversity and historical sites. Teams could develop a project to track deforestation, assess the health of local ecosystems, or monitor changes to historical monuments caused by environmental factors.

Ratnagiri Coast Monitoring

Participants will use satellite data to address challenges specific to the coastal region. This could involve creating a project to monitor coastal erosion, track changes in mangrove forests, analyze marine pollution, or develop an early warning system for storm surges and other coastal hazards.

Workshops

Exploring Our Planet: Storytelling with NASA Data

This workshop will empower participants to become storytellers using real-world data from NASA. We will guide students on a journey to explore our planet through the eyes of NASA's satellites, learning how to access and interpret a variety of open data, from Earth imagery and climate trends to topographical maps. The focus will be on transforming complex information into compelling narratives, presentations, and visual projects. Participants will learn how to build a clear, impactful story around a scientific observation, a crucial skill for communicating project ideas effectively during the hackathon.

Getting Started with NASA Open Data APIs

An introductory session on how to access and use NASA's vast collection of open data, including satellite imagery, planetary data, and astronomical observations, using developer APIs.

Space Apps Ideation & Project Planning

This workshop will guide teams through the process of brainstorming a unique project idea, defining a problem statement, and creating a feasible project plan within the hackathon's 48-hour timeline.
